About Me

Kayrene Kelley

Hi everyone! My name is Kayrene and I live about an hour from BYU-I. My husband and I have been married for 27 years. We have 6 children between us, 5 sons and 1 daughter. Our children have blessed us with 15 grandchildren and 2 great grandchildren. With the exception of our daughter, the kids live close so we get to spend a lot of time with our grandkids.

Some of my hobbies are spending time with my grandkids, driving a semi or truck, raising goats, and crocheting. We have frequent sleepovers with the grandkids and spend time camping together in the summer. I drive truck every chance I get, not as often as I would like. I have 8 adult goats and babies on the way. Last year I started chrocheting again and it has proven to be a great way to unwind and relax. I have made 10 blankets so far and half done with the 11th.

I work for the local school district as a para-professional aide. My job is to facilitate the online classes, however that means I do everthing a teacher does accept put grades in Powerschool (the councelor puts my grades in) and attend RTI meetings. This segways into my education, I am getting a degree in Applied Technology so I can get paid for what I already do. I want to teach computer related classes for the rest of my career.

The thing I love the most about BYU-I is the atmosphere. Most everyone is willing to help and work with eachother to be successful. I have been a member of the church all my life. When I was a young girl, the church/mutual program introduced song that had a profound effect on my life. It is also my favorite scripture. That song is "Love One Another" from John 13:34-35
"34: A new commandment I give unto you, That ye love one another; as I have loved you, that ye also love one another.
35: By this shall all men know that ye are my disciples, if ye have love one to another."
The other song that has had an effect on my life is "A Child's Prayer"

This looks like a fun class, and a great semester. I look forward to working with everyone.
