Everyday is an Adventure at My House!

Intro

My name is Kayrene Kelley and I have been raising goats since 2005 when my boys Kolton and Eric decided to do goat 4-H, in preparation for cow 4-H. I told both boys that they needed to do a smaller animal first. I had no idea of the journey it would lead us on.

We did not know much about goats and so I found two for sale that would be old enough to show that August. In all our infinite knowledge we chose dairy goats. Kolton bought an Alpine and Eric bought a Nubian named Snickers. After the purchase, we found out there was a show for meat goats as well and if the meat goat made weight, it would sell at the 4-H County Fair.

We were told that we could breed Snickers to a Boar goat to get meat show goats to sell. We sold Koltonâ€™s Alpine Goat and bred Snickers to get show kids. Kolton and Eric decided not to do goats again, so we were left with Snickers and her daughter, Tootsie Roll. After that year we also sold Snickers, as once a dairy goat has kids, she produces milk and has to be milked all year. The next question is "Now, what are we going to do with the goats?"

Kolton joined FFA in High School and decided to raise Boar goats for his FFA project, so we bought a purebred doe named Sapheria and he started a breeding program. As most parents know, any program your child starts, the parents start it too. We borrowed a buck and bred the does. Tootsie Roll had kids, but it took a couple of years for Sapheria to kid. After Kolton graduated, I took over the care of the goats; beginning my adventure with goats.

My children have helped my a great deal over the years to get where I am today. I was finally able to get 6 does and have a good breeding program. Now I have a mixed breed line and a purebred line. I strive to always breed my does to a purbred buck to increase the quality of the herd. My goal is to sell the kids for meat and improve the quality of my heard. My current buck is closely related enough to several of my does, so I have to sell those kids and cannot keep any doelings for breeding. Last year I purchased a new doe giving me 3 lines for herd improvement. I will be trading two more doelings in the fall to add to my herd.
